  • Notes and Recommended Reading Order:
    • The underactuated robotics opencourseware Underactuated Robotics (mit.edu) is by far the most useful material on this list, but it requires some background in controls to digest. The appendix is very helpful if you’re completely new to optimization
    • Spatial Math and Dynamics covers some of the kinematics and dynamics math needed to understand legged robots. Make sure you’re familiar with Product of Exponentials (POE) kinematics, single rigid body (SRB) dynamics, and the floating base model. Featherstone’s book [1] is one of the most commonly cited references when it comes to state-of-the-art dynamics and kinematics. MIT implements Featherstone’s algorithms in Github Repos [4] and uses it in Github Repos [2]. Drake (Github Repos [3]) also uses it behind the scenes.
    • Quadruped MPC & WBIC shows the foundation for much of the current legged robot controls work, and is ordered by publication date. The most important work from this section is in [2] and [4], where [1] lays the groundwork. See Videos [3]
    • Graphs of Convex Sets (Motion Primitives) explores computing feasible sets of trajectories (‘motion primitives’), which all stems from the idea of sequential composition [1] which says that if we can combine controllers (funnels) together, we can increase the ‘basin of attraction’ (the set of initial conditions that the controller can drive to an equilibrium). [4], [5], and Videos [2] are the most important resources in this section, but some context in optimal controls is recommended
    • Offline Optimal Control (Explicit MPC) are generally pretty cool
